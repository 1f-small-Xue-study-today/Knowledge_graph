# Skill: Design UAV Mechanical Systems in Fusion 360

## Purpose

Design UAV mechanical systems in Fusion 360, including airframe structure, aerodynamic packaging, propulsion layout, payload integration, and manufacturable assemblies.

## Inputs

- UAV mission requirements, payload mass, flight time target, size limits, and manufacturing method.
- Motor, propeller, ESC, battery, flight-controller, sensor, camera, and gimbal dimensions.
- Aerodynamic, stiffness, center-of-gravity, vibration, cooling, and maintenance constraints.
- Prototype feedback from flight tests, vibration logs, modal identification, or failure analysis.

## Procedure

1. Define the UAV layout around payload, propulsion, battery, center of gravity, and sensor visibility.
2. Build parametric Fusion 360 components for frame, arms, mounts, covers, fairings, ducts, and fastener interfaces.
3. Check assembly constraints, clearances, wiring paths, cooling paths, propeller clearance, and maintenance access.
4. Shape aerodynamic surfaces and packaging to reduce drag while preserving manufacturability.
5. Evaluate stiffness, mass distribution, resonance risk, and vibration isolation for sensors or gimbals.
6. Prepare manufacturing outputs such as drawings, STL/STEP files, CAM setup, fastener lists, and assembly notes.
7. Iterate the design after prototype tests, flight logs, modal identification, or mechanical failure analysis.

## Validation

- Confirm component fit, interference-free assembly, and correct center-of-gravity position.
- Verify propulsion clearance, payload visibility, sensor isolation, and wiring/cooling feasibility.
- Check that the structure is stiff enough for flight-control and gimbal-stabilization needs.
- Review manufacturability and repairability before final prototype release.
