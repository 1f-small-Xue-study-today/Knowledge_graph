# Fusion 360 UAV Mechanical Design

## Overview

Fusion 360 mechanical design is a strong practical capability, especially for UAV structures and aerodynamic design. This includes turning robotics, control, payload, and manufacturing constraints into clean CAD assemblies that can be prototyped, analyzed, and iterated.

For UAV work, mechanical design must balance stiffness, weight, aerodynamic shape, sensor placement, motor/propeller layout, manufacturability, and maintainability.

## Strengths

- Parametric CAD modeling in Fusion 360 for fast iteration.
- UAV frame layout, arm geometry, payload mounting, gimbal mounting, and sensor/antenna placement.
- Aerodynamic and exterior-shape design, including smooth surfaces, fairings, ducts, covers, and drag-aware packaging.
- Mechanical integration of motors, propellers, ESCs, batteries, wiring, fasteners, vibration isolation, and cooling paths.
- Design for 3D printing, CNC, laser cutting, sheet parts, and assembled prototypes.
- Assembly constraints, interference checks, exploded views, and design documentation.
- Mechanical reasoning around stiffness, mass distribution, center of gravity, resonance, vibration, and serviceability.

## UAV Design Applications

1. Design airframes that satisfy aerodynamic, structural, and payload constraints.
2. Place propulsion and control hardware while keeping wiring, cooling, and maintenance practical.
3. Tune frame stiffness and mass distribution to reduce vibration and improve flight-control performance.
4. Integrate gimbals, cameras, sensors, and protective structures without compromising balance.
5. Iterate CAD models based on test results, modal identification, and control-system behavior.
